The Orthodox Church in Belfast

About

Welcome to The Orthodox Christian Church in Belfast, a vibrant and diverse community dedicated to worship, fellowship, and spiritual growth in the tradition of the Eastern Orthodox Church. Our church serves as a spiritual home for Orthodox Christians from various cultural backgrounds, including Greek, Russian, Romanian, Serbian, and others, reflecting the rich tapestry of Orthodoxy worldwide.

Our Mission & Our Faith.

Our mission is to glorify God through worship, to teach and live the Orthodox Christian faith, and to extend the love of Christ to our community and beyond. We aim to nurture the spiritual lives of our members through the sacraments, prayer, and education, fostering a deeper understanding of the Orthodox faith and its application in daily life. Worship and Services Our liturgical life is centered around the Divine Liturgy, celebrated in both Romanian, supplemented with English. We offer regular services, including Vespers, Matins, and special services for major feast days. Our beautiful services, steeped in ancient traditions, provide a profound experience of the presence of God and the communion of saints. Community and Outreach The Orthodox Christian Church in Belfast is committed to serving our local community through various outreach programs and charitable activities. We strive to be a beacon of hope and support, offering practical assistance and spiritual encouragement to those in need. Our community events, educational programs, and social gatherings provide opportunities for fellowship and the strengthening of our bonds as a church family.

Our Services

At The Orthodox Christian Church in Belfast, our worship services are the heart of our community life, providing opportunities for spiritual renewal and connection with God and each other.

Divine Liturgy of St. John Chrysostom

The Divine Liturgy of St. John Chrysostom is celebrated every Sunday and on major feast days. This beautiful and timeless service, filled with hymns, prayers, and readings, is the primary Eucharistic liturgy of the Orthodox Church. It offers a profound experience of God's presence and the mystery of the Holy Communion.

Pasca Services

Pascha, or Easter, is the most important and joyous feast in the Orthodox calendar. Our Pascha services include the Midnight Office, the procession, the Paschal Matins, and the Divine Liturgy, culminating in the proclamation of Christ's Resurrection. These services are rich in symbolism, light, and joy, celebrating the victory of life over death.

Dormition of the Theotokos

The Feast of the Dormition of the Theotokos, celebrated on August 15th, commemorates the "falling asleep" of the Mother of God. This feast is preceded by a period of fasting and includes special services such as the Paraklesis and the Dormition Vespers, honoring the Virgin Mary and her role in the salvation history.

Nativity of Christ (Christmas)

Services include the Nativity Vigil and the Divine Liturgy, marking the birth of our Savior.

Theophany (Epiphany)

Theophany (Epiphany): Celebrated with the Great Blessing of Waters, commemorating the Baptism of Christ.

Great Lent and Holy Week

A series of services preparing us for Pascha, including the Presanctified Liturgies, the Akathist Hymn, and the intense, reflective services of Holy Week.

Exposition and Catechism of the Orthodox Christian Faith

Orthodox Christianity, rooted in the ancient traditions of the early Church, offers a rich and profound spiritual heritage. Our faith is centered on the life and teachings of Jesus Christ, the incarnate Son of God, and the ongoing work of the Holy Spirit within the Church.

The Nicene Creed, formulated at the Councils of Nicaea and Constantinople, is the foundational statement of Orthodox Christian belief, affirming the core doctrines of the Trinity, the incarnation of Christ, His resurrection, and the life of the world to come.

The Nicene Creed

Orthodox Christianity holds Holy Scripture and Holy Tradition as complementary and inseparable sources of divine revelation. The Bible is the inspired Word of God, while Tradition encompasses the teachings of the Church Fathers and the liturgical practices.

Holy Scripture and Tradition

The sacraments are central to Orthodox Christian life, conveying God's grace to the faithful. The seven primary sacraments are Baptism, Chrismation, Eucharist, Confession, Marriage, Holy Orders, and Unction.

The Sacraments

Orthodox Christian worship is richly liturgical, involving a cycle of daily, weekly, and annual services that immerse the faithful in the presence of God. The Divine Liturgy, Vespers, Matins, and various feasts and fasts structure the spiritual life of the Church.

The Liturgical Life

Icons are an integral part of Orthodox worship, serving as windows to heaven. The veneration of saints, particularly the Theotokos, is a testament to the transformative power of God's grace in human lives.

Icons and the Veneration of Saints

The ultimate goal of Orthodox Christian life is theosis, or deification, which is the process of becoming more like God through His grace. This journey involves a life of prayer, repentance, and participation in the sacraments.

Theosis: The Goal of Christian Life
